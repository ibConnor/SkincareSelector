Acne

Acne is a skin condition that is caused when the pores become clogged with oil and dead skin cells. Acne can occur on any area of the skin, but is most commonly seen on the face, chest, and back. There are a number of different treatments available for acne, including benzoyl peroxide, salicylic acid, clindamycin, retinol, and isotretinoin.

Benzoyl peroxide is a medication that is used to treat acne. It helps to clear up acne by killing the bacteria that cause it. Benzoyl peroxide is available in both over-the-counter and prescription forms.

Salicylic acid is another medication that is used to treat acne. It helps to clear up acne by removing the dead skin cells that can clog pores. Salicylic acid is available in over-the-counter and prescription forms.

Clindamycin is an antibiotic that is used to treat acne. It helps to clear up acne by killing the bacteria that cause it. Clindamycin is available in prescription forms often prescribed with benzoyl peroxide.

Retinol is a form of vitamin A that is used to treat acne. It helps to clear up acne by unclogging pores and preventing new pimples from forming. Retinol is available over-the-counter.

Isotretinoin is a medication that is used to treat severe cases of acne. It helps to clear up acne by shrinking the oil glands that produce sebum. Isotretinoin is only available through a prescription from your doctor.
