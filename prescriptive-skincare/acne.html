Acne

Acne is a common skin condition that affects many people. There are a variety of prescription medications that can be used to treat acne, including benzoyl peroxide, clindamycin, topical retinoids, topical antibiotics, azelaic acid, antibiotic tablets, and oral contraceptive pills.

Benzoyl peroxide is a medication that is used to treat acne. It helps to clear up acne by killing the bacteria that cause it. Benzoyl peroxide is available in both over-the-counter and prescription forms.

Clindamycin is an antibiotic that is used to treat acne. It helps to clear up acne by killing the bacteria that cause it. Clindamycin is available in prescription forms often prescribed with benzoyl peroxide.

Topical retinoids are medications that are applied to the skin and help to remove dead skin cells and promote the growth of new skin cells. They are often recommended for people with acne because they help to unclog pores and reduce the amount of oil on the skin.

Azelaic acid is a medication that is applied to the skin and helps to kill bacteria that can cause acne. It is often recommended for people with dark skin tones who have acne lesions.

Antibiotic tablets are medications that are taken by mouth and help to kill bacteria that can cause acne. They are often recommended for people with severe acne who have not responded to other treatments.

Oral contraceptive pills are medications that are taken by mouth and help to reduce the amount of sebum (oil) produced by the skin. They are often recommended for women who have acne.
